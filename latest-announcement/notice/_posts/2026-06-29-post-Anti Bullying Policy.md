<p><strong>Our Commitment</strong>
</p>
<p>At Regent Secondary School, we believe every child deserves to feel safe,
valued, competent and empowered in their learning journey. We are committed
to creating a caring and enabling school environment where all students
can flourish and develop positive relationships with their peers. Our approach
focuses on teaching kindness, empathy, and respect whilst addressing any
hurtful behaviour that may arise. We work together as a school community
– staff, students, and parents – to ensure everyone feels they belong.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>What is Bullying?</strong>
</p>
<p>Bullying involves persistent and intentional hurtful acts, while other
hurtful behaviours include once-off insensitive comments and physical fights.
Bullying and hurtful behaviours can take various forms, including verbal,
social, physical and cyber:</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Verbal:
Name-calling, spreading rumours, or making threats</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Social
exclusion: Deliberately leaving someone out, or encouraging others to ignore
someone</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Physical
actions: Pushing, hitting, or damaging someone's belongings</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Cyber:
Using online technology like phones, computers, or social media to hurt
or embarrass others on purpose and repeatedly</p>
<p>We take a firm stance against all forms of hurtful behaviours, from once-off,
insensitive comments to persistent intentional acts of bullying and physical
violence. This understanding guides our school in managing different forms
of hurtful behaviours.</p>
<p>We recognise that children sometimes have disagreements or say things
they don't mean. We help students understand the difference between occasional
conflicts (which are normal) and persistent bullying behaviour, addressing
each situation with care and appropriate support.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;How We Prevent Incidents</strong>
</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Take
a firm stance against hurtful behaviours and bullying</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Foster
a caring and enabling school environment, with positive teacher-student
relationships and strong peer support structure with trained student leaders</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Conduct
Character &amp; Citizenship Education lessons on empathy, conflict management,
resilience and cyber wellness</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Have
regular communications with staff and students</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>How to Report Concerns</strong>
</p>
<p>We want parents and students to feel comfortable coming to us with any
concerns. You can reach out to us in several ways:</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Direct
reporting to any staff member</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; For
students: Online confidential reporting form</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Parent
contact with teachers/school leaders</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>How We Respond</strong>
</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Immediate
physical and psychological safety measures for all students involved</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Prompt
investigation and timely updates, subject to case complexity</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Safety
plans for affected students</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Consequences
focusing on learning prosocial skills and relationship repair</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Restorative
support including school counselling, external referrals and community
&nbsp;&nbsp;resources</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Partnership
with parents throughout the process, including informing parents at the
earliest opportunity</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>How Parents Can Support</strong>
</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Talk
regularly with your child about school experiences and friendships</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Teach
respectful behaviour and reinforce healthy digital habits at home</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Monitor
your child’s online and offline interactions and activities</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Share
concerns promptly with your child’s Form Teacher or Year Head</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Work
collaboratively with school on agreed interventions</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Model
positive behaviour in your own interactions, such as being calm and respectful
in communications</p>
<p>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Use
'teachable moments' in your daily interactions with your child to explain
why certain conduct is inappropriate</p>
