<p>Our school celebrated International Friendship Day from 13 to15 April,
with engaging activities centred on the theme <em>“Forging Friendships in a Complex World.”</em> Students
explored cultural diversity and built meaningful connections with both
local and international peers.</p>
<p>Highlights included a Wefie Competition, where students expressed their
understanding of friendship through creative photographs and reflective
captions, showcasing empathy and cross-cultural appreciation. Students
also worked in groups to design 2D1N Singapore itineraries for overseas
friends, taking on the role of cultural ambassadors while deepening their
awareness of local heritage.</p>
<p>The celebration culminated in a hall programme featuring student presentations
on 3D2N travel itineraries to countries such as India, Myanmar, Malaysia
and the Philippines, highlighting diverse cultural practices. Complementing
these activities, Character and Citizenship Education lessons encouraged
reflection on empathy, respect and inclusion.</p>
<p>Through these experiences, students strengthened their global awareness
and developed a deeper appreciation for building meaningful friendships
across cultures.</p>
