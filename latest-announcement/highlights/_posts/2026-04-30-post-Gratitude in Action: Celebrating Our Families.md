<p>On 27 April, Regent Secondary School conducted a teacher-led Gratitude
lesson aimed at helping students recognise and appreciate the vital role
their family members play in their daily lives.</p>
<p>As part of the lesson, students expressed their gratitude in meaningful
ways. Some penned heartfelt thank-you messages, giving voice to sentiments
they may not often share. Others chose to show appreciation through actions,
such as assisting with household chores like cleaning and laundry, or preparing
a simple meal for their loved ones. These small but thoughtful gestures
provided valuable opportunities for students to demonstrate care in practical
ways while strengthening family bonds.</p>
<p>The session concluded with a <em>Family Time Bingo</em> activity, encouraging
students to complete a series of meaningful tasks with their family members
over the June holidays. This initiative serves as a reminder of the importance
of setting aside quality time for family despite busy schedules, and reinforces
the idea that appreciation can be expressed not only through words, but
also through consistent, thoughtful actions.
