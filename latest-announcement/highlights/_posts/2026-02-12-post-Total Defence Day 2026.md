<p><strong><u>Together We Keep Singapore Strong: Total Defence Day 2026</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Every year on 15 February, Singapore marks Total Defence Day. This year,
Regent Secondary School commemorated the occasion through three key segments
that reinforced the importance of resilience in today’s rapidly changing
world.</p>
<p>Students first engaged with the Total Defence Lesson Package, where they
explored how threats today extend beyond traditional warfare to include
digital and psychological challenges. Through discussions and scenario-based
activities, students brainstormed ways to respond to modern crises, strengthening
their awareness of how every individual plays a role in keeping Singapore
safe and united.</p>
<p>As part of Exercise SG Ready on 6 February 2026, the school also simulated
a large- scale digital connectivity disruption. During the exercise, students
learnt how to identify misinformation, practise alternative communication
methods and understand the importance of Digital Defence. The activity
highlighted how staying calm, thinking critically and supporting one another
are essential during times of disruption.</p>
<p>The spirit of Total Defence was especially evident during the Uniformed
Groups parade held on 10 February. Members of the National Police Cadet
Corps (NPCC), Red Cross Youth (RCY), Boys’ Brigade and Girls’ Brigade demonstrated
discipline, unity and commitment to service. This was followed by student
presentations reinforcing the message that defending Singapore is a shared
responsibility, with every action contributing to a stronger nation.</p>
<p>Through these experiences, our students were reminded that resilience,
vigilance and unity remain key to keeping Singapore strong for the future.
By staying prepared for disruptions—both physical and digital—we ensure
that Singapore remains a safe and sovereign home for all.</p>
