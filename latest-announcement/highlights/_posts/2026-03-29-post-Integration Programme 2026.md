<p><strong><u>Integration Programme 2026: Building Connections Across Cultures</u></strong>
</p>
<p>On 5 March 2026, Regent Secondary School organised an integration programme
aimed at fostering connection, collaboration and a strong sense of belonging
among students from diverse cultural backgrounds. The programme brought
together international and local students through a series of engaging
and meaningful activities.</p>
<p>The day began with icebreaker activities that created a welcoming and
inclusive environment. Through games such as BINGO and small group sharing,
students interacted in a relaxed setting which eased the initial awkwardness
and built rapport among participants. These early interactions laid the
foundation for open communication and mutual respect.</p>
<p>Building on this growing sense of connection, students had the opportunity
to bond over a selection of local food. This informal segment encouraged
conversations as students shared personal stories and cultural experiences
while enjoying familiar dishes. It provided a meaningful platform for students
to deepen their understanding of one another and appreciate diverse perspectives.</p>
<p>A key highlight of the programme was the Linocut printing activity where
students explored creativity in a hands-on setting. Guided through the
process of designing, carving and printing, students expressed themselves
while developing patience and focus. Working alongside one another, they
shared ideas, offered feedback and celebrated each other’s creations, fostering
a supportive and collaborative atmosphere.</p>
<p>Overall, the integration programme combined interactive, cultural and
creative elements to promote meaningful engagement between international
and local students. Students left with stronger relationships, enhanced
communication skills and a greater sense of community, making the programme
both impactful and memorable.</p>
